Tax 101

Facts About Tax

Taxation is Ancient: Taxes have been collected for thousands of years. Ancient civilizations like the Egyptians, Greeks, and Romans all had systems of taxation.

Income Tax: The Income Tax Act of India governs the levy and collection of income tax. Individuals and businesses are required to pay income tax based on their income and profits, respectively.

Tax Filing Deadline: In India, the deadline for filing income tax returns is usually July 31st for individuals and September 30th for businesses. However, the government may extend these deadlines under certain circumstances.

Tax Slabs: India has a progressive income tax system with multiple tax slabs. The slabs and rates vary based on the individual's or business's income level.

GST (Goods and Services Tax): India introduced the Goods and Services Tax (GST) on July 1, 2017, replacing a complex system of indirect taxes. GST is a value-added tax that applies to the supply of goods and services across the country.

Global Taxation:Some multinational corporations use complex strategies to minimize their tax liability, leading to debates about global tax fairness.

Tax Deductions:Taxpayers can often reduce their taxable income by claiming deductions for things like mortgage interest, medical expenses, and charitable contributions.
